  - type: context
    label: Project Context
    lead: true
    text: >-
      Wallyx is a new consumer rewards app for Nayax — a global payments company
      whose card readers and vending machines sit inside businesses everywhere.
      The idea: reward everyday shoppers with points and perks every time they
      pay at a Nayax device, creating a bottom-up consumer demand that pulls
      businesses toward acquiring Nayax. Moveo ran the full process — strategy,
      brand, and app design. The brand had to unify very different audiences
      under one "umbrella" concept, and launch Nayax's first B2C brand inside a
      house that until now held only B2B brands.

  # ===================== STEP 1 =====================
  - type: heading
    text: "Step 1 — Strategy & Branding"

  - type: process
    label: Process & Goals
    title: Process
    text: >-
      Discovery came first: a market overview of local and global competitors,
      interviews with key stakeholders, and up to five focus groups spanning
      very different audiences — from soldiers to hospital staff to everyday
      commuters — distilled into insights and personas. From there: brand
      values, naming and value proposition, and a visual identity — logo,
      colour, typography and iconography — captured in a brandbook.

  - type: process
    title: Goals
    text: >-
      Articulate the brand story and an "umbrella concept" that unifies the
      product's diverse audiences and use cases; position Wallyx within the
      digital-wallet and money-saving ecosystem; and build a visual identity and
      an app design system that bring that story to life across every touchpoint.

  - type: heading
    text: "Challenges & Solutions"

  - type: challenge
    label: "A positioning challenge"
    challenge: >-
      The product sat in the tension between three categories at once — a
      financial/wallet app, a gamified rewards app, and a loyalty program. Each
      carries a different promise and feel, and leaning too far into any one of
      them would misrepresent the product and its audience.
    solution: >-
      We anchored the brand in the emotional payoff rather than the money.
      Wallyx isn't a cold finance app or a plastic points card — it's the small,
      everyday "treat yourself" win: a little moment of delight along the way.
      The concept — "enjoy the moments along the way" — framed rewards as
      feeling, not maths.

  - type: images
    items: [./images/key-visual.png]
    caption: "‘Enjoy the moments along the way’ — the brand's everyday-moments key visual"

  - type: challenge
    label: "A branded-house challenge"
    challenge: >-
      Nayax had only ever built B2B brands. Wallyx was its first consumer-facing
      brand, and had to feel warm, playful and human — while still clearly
      belonging to the Nayax parent and its existing visual world.
    solution: >-
      A distinct, friendly B2C identity built on Nayax's yellow-and-black, with
      a playful hand-drawn "W" mark. The name Wallyx carries it too: drawn from
      the Korean word for wallet (Jigab), it hides "Wallet," reads as a cute
      character (echoing Wall-E), and ends in "yx" to tie it back to Nayax.

  # ===================== STEP 2 =====================
  - type: heading
    text: "Step 2 — App Design"

  - type: process
    label: Process
    title: Process
    text: >-
      As design lead on the app phase, I translated the approved visual language
      into product: the home-screen UI, a component library and design system,
      and a style guide — plus the launch creative that carries the brand into
      social, out-of-home and merch.

  - type: heading
    text: "Challenges & Solutions"

  - type: challenge
    label: "A tone challenge"
    challenge: >-
      The app had to feel like a treat and a game — light, rewarding, a little
      magical — without undermining the trust a product that touches people's
      money has to earn. Too playful reads as unserious; too financial kills the
      delight.
    solution: >-
      A warm, energetic interface — yellow-forward, rounded, and character-led —
      with clear, legible reward mechanics. Gamified moments celebrate each small
      win, while the underlying structure stays simple and trustworthy, so the
      app feels delightful and dependable at the same time.
