  - type: context
    label: Project Context
    lead: true
    text: >-
      The Snap-Off Knife is a stainless-steel and brass utility knife built for
      a standard replaceable snap-off blade. The low friction between the steel
      and brass parts creates a smooth, controlled sliding movement. The project
      was a platform for exploring metal — its form, composition, color, and
      weight — with a simple aim: to bring joy to an everyday product through
      clean, functional design.

  - type: process
    label: Process, Goals & Users
    title: Process
    text: >-
      The project was run as material research. Metal was explored as the
      subject itself — form, composition, color, and weight — and resolved
      through development drawings and component design into a refined
      steel-and-brass tool built around a standard replaceable blade.

  - type: process
    title: Goals
    text: >-
      Take an ordinary, near-disposable everyday object — the snap-off utility
      knife — and bring it joy and quality through clean, functional design. Use
      the project as an exploration of metal as a material, and achieve a
      movement that feels smooth and controlled in the hand.

  - type: process
    title: Users
    text: >-
      Anyone who reaches for a utility knife day to day — and, more
      specifically, people who value a well-made, pleasurable version of an
      everyday tool.

  - type: heading
    text: "Challenges & Solutions"

  - type: challenge
    label: "An elevation challenge"
    challenge: >-
      A snap-off knife is a cheap, purely utilitarian object most people never
      think about. The challenge was to make it a refined, pleasurable thing to
      own and use without losing its everyday function.
    solution: >-
      A considered material pairing — stainless steel with brass — and a clean,
      functional form turn a disposable tool into a tactile object, treating
      metal's color, weight, and composition as part of the design rather than
      an afterthought.

  - type: images
    items:

    caption: "Components / exploded view"

  - type: challenge
    label: "A mechanism challenge"
    challenge: >-
      A utility knife lives or dies on its action: the blade must slide and lock
      smoothly and feel controlled, while still accepting a standard replaceable
      snap-off blade.
    solution: >-
      The steel-and-brass pairing does double duty — the low friction between
      the two metals produces a smooth, controlled movement, engineered around a
      standard replaceable blade so the refinement never compromises everyday
      practicality.
