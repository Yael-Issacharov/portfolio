  - type: context
    label: Project Context
    lead: true
    text: >-
      Uno is a premium eyewear collection designed for the Barner brand. Its
      guiding idea inverts the usual logic of eyewear: a frame to match each
      consumer, instead of one frame to match all consumers. The frames are made
      from sustainable bio-acetate, and the collection is planned across two
      seasons — blue-light and prescription models first, sunglasses second —
      each model offered in three colorways.

  - type: process
    label: Process, Goals & Users
    title: Process
    text: >-
      The work ran from concept to production-ready collection: a design
      concept, a trend board and a curated color card built on trend forecasting
      with a timeless intent, sketching, and 3D design — resolved into named
      models and colorways. As product manager, the collection was structured by
      season (FW22 optical, SS23 sun), model, and color.

  - type: images

  - type: process
    title: Goals
    text: >-
      Deliver a premium, sustainable eyewear collection for Barner that fits the
      individual personality of the brand's consumer rather than a generic
      average. Simplify each frame's shape to its essence — a look that is
      current and fashionable yet personal — and give the brand a coherent,
      seasonally structured range it can bring to market.

  - type: process
    title: Users
    text: >-
      Barner's consumers — individuals who want eyewear that reflects their own
      personality: blue-light and prescription wearers first, sunglasses buyers
      in the following season.

  - type: heading
    text: "Challenges & Solutions"

  - type: challenge
    label: "A personalization challenge"
    challenge: >-
      The brief asked for frames that feel individual — a frame to match each
      consumer — while the models still had to read as one coherent brand
      collection rather than a set of unrelated objects.
    solution: >-
      A holistic, essential interpretation of current trends: each frame's shape
      simplified to its core, then differentiated through a curated color card.
      Distinct models — Palermo, Chelsea, a round frame — give personality range
      while a shared design language holds the collection together.

  - type: images
    items:
      - ./images/group.jpg
    caption: "Collection group shot"

  - type: challenge
    label: "A trend-versus-timeless challenge"
    challenge: >-
      Eyewear is fashion, so the collection had to feel on-trend; but a premium,
      sustainable product should not date in a season, and colors and forms
      needed staying power.
    solution: >-
      Colors and effects were selected through trend forecasting but chosen with
      a timeless intention, and the frames were made from sustainable
      bio-acetate — so the range feels contemporary while the material and
      palette are built to last.
