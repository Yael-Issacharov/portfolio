  - type: context
    label: Project Context
    lead: true
    text: >-
      Marc. is a limited series of one-off ceramic cups, made for the Artesania
      Catalunya souvenir shop and conceived around Catalan culture with a local
      craftsman. Marc means "frame" in Catalan — a rigid structure that
      surrounds something, like a door or window. Each cup is decorated with a
      "mark of time," a gesture toward a traditional Catalan glazing style,
      framed at the center of an otherwise contemporary object.

  - type: process
    label: Process, Goals & Users
    title: Process
    text: >-
      The project moved from concept to craft. It began with a text by the poet
      Joan Brossa and an observation on how time is perceived while a piece is
      made and read; developed a primary concept — framing the time that passed
      in the product's present; ran cultural research into Catalan ceramics; and
      was produced on the wheel with ceramist Carmen Balada as a series of six
      one-offs.

  - type: process
    title: Goals
    text: >-
      Create a souvenir genuinely rooted in Catalan culture and made with a
      local craftsman, for Artesania Catalunya. Express the region's ceramics
      heritage in a contemporary object — using clay, a material that
      "remembers" its making and its origin — and frame a traditional green
      glaze at the heart of each cup as a mark of time.

  - type: process
    title: Users
    text: >-
      Visitors and buyers at the Artesania Catalunya souvenir shop who want a
      culturally authentic, craft-made Catalan object rather than a generic
      souvenir.

  - type: heading
    text: "Challenges & Solutions"

  - type: challenge
    label: "A concept-to-object challenge"
    challenge: >-
      The idea was literary and abstract — Brossa's text, the perception of
      time, the notion of a "frame." It risked staying conceptual; it had to
      become a real, usable cup that still carried the meaning.
    solution: >-
      The abstract "frame" was made physical: a traditional green-colored glaze
      framed at the center of the contemporary cup as a visible "mark of time,"
      so the concept is read directly in the object rather than explained beside
      it.

  - type: images
    items:
      - ./images/carmen-wheel.png
    caption: "Carmen Balada at the wheel"

  - type: challenge
    label: "An authenticity challenge"
    challenge: >-
      A souvenir about Catalan culture can easily become a cliché. It needed to
      be credibly local in both reference and making, not a surface motif
      applied to a generic form.
    solution: >-
      Cultural research into Catalan ceramics grounded the reference, and the
      cups were co-created with Catalan ceramist Carmen Balada — thrown on the
      lathe in local stoneware clay as six genuine one-offs, each unique.
