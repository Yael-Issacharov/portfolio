  - type: context
    label: Project Context
    lead: true
    text: |
      Cye is a cybersecurity company (founded 2012, Israel) that pairs an AI-native platform with an elite team of human experts, delivering cyber exposure management — CTEM with cyber risk quantification in financial terms — to enterprises worldwide.

      They came to us because their brand and website no longer reflected the company: a conventional B2B SaaS marketing site that read like every other security vendor and contradicted Cye's AI-native position. We ran two connected workstreams — a full rebrand, and a new website designed to behave like an LLM chat app.

  - type: heading
    text: "Step 1 — Strategy & Brand"

  - type: process
    label: Process, Goals & Values
    title: Process
    text: >-
      The brand workstream moved from foundation to system: brand goals and
      values, a design concept ("The Radar System"), visual directions, a new
      logo, then a full brand language — colour, typography, iconography,
      photography, and platform representation — and its applications across
      product, social, and slides.

    items:
      - ./images/brand-process.svg
    caption: "Brand workstream: goals/values → concept → logo → language → applications"

  - type: process
    title: Goals
    text: >-
      Short term: create a clear, confident brand window that accurately
      reflects who Cye is today, establishing it as a credible, enterprise-grade
      partner with the experience and reliability of a market leader. Long term:
      build consistent, recognizable awareness — Cye understood, trusted, and
      referenced across the market, including analyst and enterprise
      conversations — aligned to the CTEM category and Gartner standards.

  - type: process
    title: Values
    text: >-
      Clarity, Confidence, Mastery, Partnership, Reliability, and Creativity — a
      personality that should feel competent, mature, flexible, sincere, and
      authentic, balancing seriousness with optimism and confidence.

    items:
      - ./images/values.png

  - type: heading
    text: "Challenges & Solutions"

  - type: challenge
    label: "A concept challenge"
    challenge: >-
      In a landscape of cyber noise, the brand needed a single idea that
      captured how Cye creates clarity and directs decisions — one strong enough
      to carry across both the visual identity and the digital product
      experience.
    solution: >-
      The concept "The Radar System," expressed through the tagline "The Right
      Action." Like tactical navigation, Cye scans the whole attack surface,
      isolates the risks that actually matter, and points to what to do next —
      Scan, Identify, Understand, Act.

  - type: images
    items:
      - ./images/radar-concept.png
    caption: "Brand concept — The Radar System"

  - type: images
    items:
      - ./images/radar-motif.svg
    caption: "Motif: Scan · Identify · Understand · Act"

  - type: challenge
    label: "A positioning challenge"
    challenge: >-
      The identity had to project the maturity and reliability of an enterprise
      market leader in the CTEM category, while still signaling the creative
      agility of an AI-native company solving concrete risk — without either
      register undercutting the other or drifting into generic AI-startup
      visuals.
    solution: >-
      A restrained, enterprise-grade system — Aeonik type, a deep slate-and-cyan
      core palette with sharper accent colours — paired with the radar motif and
      its motion. The result reads as both credible and forward-looking, mature
      but not static, native to an AI product rather than applied on top of one.

  # ===================== STEP 2 =====================
  - type: heading
    text: "Step 2 — Website (UX/UI: A Site That Behaves Like an LLM)"

  - type: process
    label: Process, Goals & Audiences
    title: Process
    text: >-
      The website workstream began with research — a competitive analysis of ten
      cybersecurity sites and market research on leading AI apps — then moved to
      website goals and persona types, homepage structure and wireframe, the
      Smart Search feature (capability needs, capability list, main screen,
      active chat, content-to-chat), and design.

  - type: images
    items:
      - ./images/website-process.svg
    caption: "Website workstream: research → goals/personas → wireframe → smart search → design"

  - type: process
    title: Goals
    text: >-
      Lead engagement and conversion — guide users toward contact and
      decision-making; market positioning — Cye as a strategic partner for
      cybersecurity decision-making; content accessibility and clarity — users
      easily find and understand what's relevant to them; a search-based,
      AI-driven experience that adapts to user intent; and a scalable,
      future-ready platform. Long term: replace the existing website with a
      simplified, structured experience.

  - type: process
    title: Audiences
    text: >-
      Primary: enterprise security leader (CISO / Head of Security) and
      technical evaluator (security architect / risk officer). Secondary: group
      / multi-entity security leader (Group CISO), reseller partner, and MSSP
      partner. Tertiary: private-equity operating partner.

  - type: images
    items:
      - ./images/personas.png
    caption: "Persona types & experience priority"

  - type: heading
    text: "Challenges & Solutions"

  - type: challenge
    label: "A content challenge"
    challenge: >-
      A conventional site holds its value in static pages — how it works,
      methodology, case studies, about. The task was to turn that content into
      an AI-native, question-driven experience without losing the substance and
      proof that security buyers rely on.
    solution: >-
      Content becomes chat. Static pages are re-expressed as structured in-chat
      responses, surfaced when a user asks — company overview, differentiation,
      methodology, credibility signals, and proof points delivered in context
      rather than hunted for across a menu.

  - type: images
    items:
      - ./images/content-to-chat.svg
    caption: "Static pages become live, contextual responses"

  - type: challenge
    label: "A conceptual challenge"
    challenge: >-
      The defining decision was to make a B2B cybersecurity website behave like
      an LLM chat app — Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity — not a traditional
      SaaS marketing site, without losing the authority and proof enterprise
      security buyers require before they trust a vendor.
    solution: >-
      We audited ten competitor sites — none conversational — and ten leading AI
      apps for their entry and artifact patterns. Those were mapped onto Cye: a
      prompt-first homepage, guided prompts, and generated answers, framed with
      enterprise-grade visuals and trust signals so the experience reads as
      credible, not casual.

  - type: images
    items:
      - ./images/rationale.png
    caption: "Why chat-first — the rationale"

  - type: challenge
    label: "A personalization challenge"
    challenge: >-
      The site serves several audiences at different priorities — CISO,
      technical evaluator, group CISO, reseller and MSSP partners, and PE
      operating partner — each arriving with different questions and depth. The
      experience had to fit each of them without fragmenting into separate
      sites.
    solution: >-
      The Smart Search feature detects intent from the first query and adapts
      tone, depth, and routing to the inferred persona — without asking users to
      self-identify — while keeping one consistent brand and system across the
      site.

  - type: images
    items:
      - ./images/smart-search.png
    caption: "The Smart Search feature"
